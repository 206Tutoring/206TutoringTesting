COLLEGE COUNSELING FROM WHITMAN TO WASHINGTON TO WESLEYAN

I grew up in Seattle and went east for College, graduating from Columbia in 2009. In New York, I found, college admissions had become extremely corporate, and the extortionary costs put all kids, even wealthy kids, under huge amounts of stress — if you’re having that much spent on you, the thought process goes, you better get in.

My college counseling is in opposition to that. I don’t put demands upon my students’ time — I free it up. I don’t tell my students that they “have to go to Harvard” or go “Yale or bust” — I show them that there are many places they’d be happy, and those may simply be two of them. I don’t tell them there’s only one way to write an essay or fill out an app — I show them how to fit the form into their own lives, and familiarize them with concepts like sample size and variance that will keep them in good stead beyond college and into adult life.

Here’s what I bring to the table:

— Admission to Columbia from public high school, with a minimum of test prep and college counseling
— Keeping in touch with friends, and visiting them at their high schools and colleges from LA to Boston
— Five years experience as an editor; published author with exceptional writing skills

Here’s why that bests what my competitors have to offer:

— Younger age (26) and peer group gives me far greater insight into the modern college-admissions process than older counselors
— Less entrenchment means my perspective is fresher and unencumbered by market dependence
— Less boosterism for universities; more realistic, less PR-style appraisals of colleges and college life

Here’s some topics we’ll cover to help you narrow down your search:

— Scholarships / "bubbling for dollars"
— Why rankings aren’t all that relevant, except for that other people think they are
— Liberal-arts vs. small university vs. large university
— The ‘rule of first impressions’
— Keeping your options open within the school
— Keeping your options open for all schools
— ‘Bubbling for dollars’ and other ways to reduce cost
— Prestige’s upsides and downsides
— Why the East Coast is so alluring
— On-campus vs. off-campus life
— Why your intended major doesn’t matter much

Finally, here’s how we’ll take your application from “barely started” to “accepted”:

— Finishing applications efficiently and quickly
— Early-decision vs. early-action
— Essay-writing and essay-editing for hassled and overworked teens
— Getting through applications, one at a time, over the course of a few months
— Why to apply to the UW in addition to Harvard, so to speak
— Primary sources on college admissions from Slate, Salon, the New York Times, the New Yorker, and more
— Troubleshooting stress
