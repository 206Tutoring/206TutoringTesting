HOW AND WHY I TEACH MATH

I can teach you a year’s worth of math in under ten hours. Here’s how:

Math is the subject taught worst in school. I strongly believe this is due to math being approached as a series of arbitrary and unrelated formulas and theorems, to be committed rotely to memory. In reality, it is the study of patterns that relate to one another and every other subject. Most recently printed math books have little merit, beyond being brightly colored; they succeed at confusing and discouraging even brilliant students, which is a shame, as (and this is true for math more so than any other subject) nearly every math class could be aced by a majority of students.

I would like to prove this to you! My students deserve to understand math efficiently and comprehensively, so that math class is not a cause for anxiety and frustration, but confidence and discovery. My teaching method starts at the beginning and works its way up, speedily and conceptually, to whatever material the teacher covering (or would like to cover). This way, the current material is more deeply understood, previous topics will become harder to forget, and upcoming topics will be swiftly grasped.

In contrast to English classes, most math classes consist of no more than 20 concepts for an entire year; I can cover that much material in no longer than 10 hours. A chapter review should take little more than one hour. I'm proficient in the use of a graphing calculator and other labor-saving devices, and I can present strategies for coping with inordinate homework loads and the other stresses of being a secondary-school student.
