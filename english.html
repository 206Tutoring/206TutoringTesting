THE KINDS OF ENGLISH, READING, AND WRITING LESSONS I GIVE

In school I was always told that ‘everyone needs math,’ and, though this is true, everyone needs reading and writing far more. With the rise of the Internet, the increasing difficulty and subjectivity of college admissions, and the competition for scarce jobs hinging on résumés, interviews, and communication skills, your proficiency in reading and writing is more critical than ever before.

Reading well is essential to living well. Reading for school can be tedious, but I can help you do it more quickly and with greater understanding and memory, by discovering how it relates to other school subjects and your life. Metaphors like ‘reading the situation’ or ‘hand-reading’ or 'reading music’ or ‘mixed messages’ are no accident; good reading is inseparable from good judgment. Improving your language skills will also improve your thought processes. As a literature student at Columbia, I chewed through a big chunk of the Western canon, so I see reading and writing as a shortcut and aid to human experience.

Writing and reading well are marketable skills. The SAT, ACT, and GRE all have some reading and writing components, as do most college and job applications. Fastidious reading comprehension and clear expression of ideas will make you look smart, and will empower you to excel in any private, public, or social situation, while granting you educational and economic opportunities for the rest of your life.

Not only will reading and writing well make you look smart, they’ll actually make you smarter. Reading well will let you make connections between seemingly dissimilar ideas, allowing you to discern patterns in people's behavior while avoiding making the same mistake twice. An expansive vocabulary will enable you to call things what they are. I could keep going, but I can also teach you brevity!
