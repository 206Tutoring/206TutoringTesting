FROM THE BEGINNING TO ...

I’m a writer. When I was a student, I couldn’t help but get published – I learned to write for an audience on the Garfield HS Messenger, and honed my skills further as a staff writer and columnist for the Columbia Spectator and its literary-magazine sister publication.

But when I graduated, everything dried up. This is a common experience! I eventually realized that this was because everyone in the real world was a "writer," and that I couldn’t do without the self-promotion that was so unnatural and difficult for me. It took me some time to figure out how to deal with it, during which my creativity suffered. Whether you’re a housewife, a CEO, a self-employed creative type, or all of the above, I want to help you avoid the same, and have your writing get the attention it ought to be getting. That’s why I’ve started Classes in Writing for Publication!

My credentials:

— I’ve written and iterated several long-form projects, including a 250-page novel, and have published hundreds of articles from New York literary magazines to online hobby sites that gross seven figures.
— As an undergraduate, I interned for the Columbia Journalism Review and conducted literary research for C. David Heymann, an author of popular biographies, in addition to working as a columnist on the campus daily, serving as a copy-editor for a literary magazine, and a handful of other, smaller obligations.
— I maintain dozens of contacts in the publishing, magazine, and newspaper industries, whose advice I am happy to draw upon and feature over the course of my class!
— After college, I dedicated myself to longer projects, but have recently been paid to write regularly for two large and profitable websites.

Ideal for:

— Recent university graduates who find themselves struggling to write or write for publication after college
— Professionals who want to improve their style and confidence for official communiqués, etc.
— Writers, bloggers, Tweeters, et al. who want to expand their Internet audience
— Anyone who wants to come out and meet more interesting people!

Topics include:

— Tooting your own horn — I know a lot of creative minds find this distasteful, or at least difficult, and I completely understand. I can help. Between my contacts in the publishing industry and my experience getting high-schoolers to write college essays, I know why it’s hard, and I know what works.
— Editing, but not overediting, to fit the tone of your target publication
— Using social media, as well as the appropriate online advertising, to promote yourself
— Developing contacts who can help get you what you want, even if you dislike them
— The compromise implicit in all individualism
