EDUCATIONAL PHILOSOPHY

The experience of education in 21st-century America is too often anxiety and frustration and insomnia. Too many tutors exacerbate these stresses, by creating a high-stakes environment, assigning homework or worksheets of their own, demanding the wrong things of their students, or otherwise misunderstanding their role.

The way I see it, my job is the opposite of that.

I tutor because I want to work against the worst aspects of school (and music lessons), and embody the best ones. I tutor to make school more worthwhile and more fun for my students. I tutor to relieve anxiety and frustration and stress. I tutor to help my students spend less time on homework, and more time on the things they love to do. I do this by promoting a relaxing and comfortable environment, encouraging my students to ask questions about the material (or whatever else they’re interested in), and cultivating a disposition for curiosity, which allows them to feel like themselves, put the problems at hand in the right context, and focus in on them.

WHY MY LESSONS WORK

In my experience, a relaxed tutoring environment is both a hugely more pleasant experience for my students, and a far more effective way to get them motivated and learning; the numeric gains on GPA and test scores, and the subjective gains in just ‘feeling better,’ are more than enough to convince me of this.

In school, everyone has to do things they don’t want to do; again, my job is to put those things in context, and give my students a way to deal with the difficulties of school. That way, I can help them master their schoolwork, and they can discover what interests them and be more alert to all the possibilities of life — and this is the true purpose of education.
